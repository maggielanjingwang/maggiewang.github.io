Now UW · Seattle

I'm a PhD student in Biomedical Informatics at the University of Washington, working at the intersection of large language models, clinical evidence, and real-world data. My research develops LLM and agentic systems that retrieve, structure, and synthesize medical knowledge, from clinical literature to electronic health records.

Before UW, I was a PhD student in Biostatistics at the George Washington University and earned my B.A. in Data Science from UC Berkeley. I've built data and modeling pipelines across the American Heart Association's registries, large-scale systematic reviews, and clinical LLM research, all grounded in a statistical foundation of meta-analysis, study design, and causal reasoning.

Interests LLMs & agentic systems for healthcare · clinical NLP & retrieval · evidence synthesis · EHR & real-world data · computational medicine · biostatistics

01 · Research

What I work on

Four threads, one goal: making medical evidence machine-readable and clinically actionable.

◆

LLMs for Clinical Literature Retrieval

Evaluation benchmarks and LLM-based retrieval for clinically relevant oncology literature, using structured clinical schemas (treatment line, biomarkers, patient context) and guideline-informed dynamic filters that improve retrieval precision.

LLMsRetrievalOncologyAMIA 2026
◇

Agentic Evidence Synthesis

End-to-end clinical LLM pipelines and agentic literature-review workflows for automated study screening, clinical context schema design, and statistical analysis for multi-source evidence synthesis, with human-in-the-loop multi-agent systems.

AgentsEvidence SynthesisHuman-in-the-loop
▣

EHR & Real-World Evidence

Scalable EHR infrastructure with an agent-based querying layer for cohort construction and longitudinal analysis, quantifying guideline adoption and the lag from clinical evidence to real-world practice, and structuring notes into outcomes (OS, PFS).

EHRCohortsRWE
▤

Biostatistics for Epidemiology & Clinical Trials

Meta-analysis, meta-regression, missing-data imputation, and sensitivity & subgroup analyses for the epidemiology, and multi-agentic, human-in-the-loop systems for systematic literature reviews of clinical trials.
